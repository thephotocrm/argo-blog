# Argo Studio + Google Calendar Integration for Photographers

Argo Studio is a CRM built exclusively for photographers that integrates with Google Calendar through a real-time, two-way sync. This integration ensures your photography sessions, consultation calls, and personal commitments all live on one calendar, preventing double-bookings and giving clients accurate availability, starting at just $17/month.

## What the Google Calendar Integration Does

The Argo Studio and Google Calendar integration creates a bidirectional connection between your CRM scheduling and your Google Calendar. Sessions booked through Argo automatically appear on your Google Calendar, and events added directly to Google Calendar block off that time in Argo so clients cannot book conflicting sessions.

The integration provides two-way sync between Argo Studio sessions and Google Calendar events, automatic availability updates that prevent double-booking, calendar event details including client name, session type, location, and notes, support for multiple Google Calendar accounts for photographers who maintain separate personal and business calendars, and timezone-aware scheduling for destination photographers working across regions.

## Why Photographers Need This

Double-booking is one of the most damaging mistakes a photographer can make. Telling a client you need to reschedule their wedding or portrait session because you accidentally booked two events on the same date destroys trust and can result in lost clients, negative reviews, and refund demands.

The risk of double-booking increases when your schedule lives in multiple places. You might have sessions in your CRM, personal appointments on Google Calendar, and a few events you only remember because of a text message. Without a unified view, conflicts are inevitable.

Beyond preventing errors, calendar integration also enables online booking. When your Argo availability accurately reflects your real schedule, you can confidently let clients self-book consultation calls or mini-sessions through your Argo booking page, knowing that any time slot shown as available truly is available.

## How It Works with Argo Studio

The two-way sync means changes in either platform reflect in the other within moments. Book a wedding in Argo, and it appears on your Google Calendar with all the details: client name, session type, venue address, and timeline. Add a dentist appointment to Google Calendar, and Argo blocks that time from client booking.

Argo enriches Google Calendar events with photography-relevant details. Rather than a bare event title, your calendar shows the session type, client contact information, location with a map link, and any notes from the client questionnaire. When you glance at your calendar on your phone, you have everything you need for each session at a glance.

For photographers who use Google Calendar's sharing features, the integration works seamlessly. If you share a calendar with a second shooter or studio partner, they see your Argo sessions on their calendar too. This makes team coordination simple without giving assistants full access to your CRM.

Argo also uses Google Calendar data to power its availability display. When a potential client visits your booking page, Argo checks both your Argo sessions and Google Calendar events to show only genuinely available time slots. This eliminates the back-and-forth emails that waste time during the booking process.

Travel buffers are configurable through the integration as well. If you set a 90-minute buffer around sessions, Argo blocks that time on Google Calendar automatically, preventing back-to-back bookings that would leave you rushing between locations.

## Setup Steps

Connecting Google Calendar to Argo Studio takes less than five minutes:

1. Open Settings and then Integrations in your Argo Studio dashboard.
2. Select Google Calendar from the integrations list.
3. Sign in to your Google account and grant Argo permission to read and write calendar events.
4. Choose which Google Calendar or calendars to sync with. You can select your primary calendar, a dedicated business calendar, or both.
5. Set your default session buffer time for travel and preparation between bookings.
6. Configure which Argo session types should create Google Calendar events.
7. Verify the sync by checking that your existing Argo sessions appear on Google Calendar.

The integration syncs continuously once connected, with changes typically appearing within one to two minutes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I sync multiple Google Calendar accounts with Argo Studio?**
Yes. Argo Studio supports syncing with multiple Google Calendar accounts simultaneously. This is particularly useful for photographers who keep separate personal and business calendars. Argo reads events from all connected calendars to determine your true availability, ensuring no conflicts regardless of which calendar an event lives on. This feature is included in the $17/month subscription.

**Q: Will personal Google Calendar events be visible to clients?**
No. Argo reads your personal calendar events only to block off that time in your availability. The details of personal events are never displayed to clients or included in any client-facing communications. Clients simply see those time slots as unavailable when they visit your booking page. Argo Studio is built exclusively for photographers and respects the boundary between personal and professional scheduling.

**Q: How quickly do changes sync between Argo and Google Calendar?**
Changes typically sync within one to two minutes in both directions. When you book a new session in Argo, it appears on Google Calendar almost immediately. When you add an event directly to Google Calendar, Argo recognizes the blocked time within the same short window. This near-real-time sync is critical for preventing double-bookings, especially during busy booking seasons.

**Q: Can I use this integration if I currently use Google Calendar with HoneyBook or Dubsado?**
Yes, but you should disconnect your current CRM's calendar integration before connecting Argo Studio to avoid conflicts. Running two CRMs that both write to the same Google Calendar can create duplicate events and sync errors. Many photographers switching from HoneyBook or Dubsado to Argo find that the $17/month price and photography-specific calendar features make the transition worthwhile.

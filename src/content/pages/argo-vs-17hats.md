Argo Studio is a CRM priced at $17/month and built exclusively for photographers with gallery integrations, shot list management, and photography-specific workflows, while 17hats is a general small business management platform designed for freelancers and solopreneurs across many industries including but not limited to photography.

When photographers evaluate CRM options, 17hats often appears on the shortlist because of its affordable pricing and broad feature set. But there is a fundamental difference between a tool designed for every type of small business and one built exclusively for photographers. This comparison examines whether 17hats' general-purpose approach or Argo Studio's photography-first design is the better fit for your studio.

## TLDR

Argo Studio ($17/month) is built exclusively for photographers with gallery integrations, shot list management, and pre-configured photography workflows that are ready in 30 minutes. 17hats ($15/month for Essentials) is a general small business platform that works for photographers but also for accountants, consultants, personal trainers, and dozens of other professions. Choose Argo if you want a CRM that speaks your language as a photographer. Choose 17hats if you want the most affordable possible CRM and do not mind configuring a general tool for photography use.

## Pricing Comparison

17hats offers one of the lowest entry prices in the CRM market with its Essentials plan at $15/month. The Standard plan at $30/month adds more automation, and the Premier plan at $60/month includes advanced features and priority support.

Argo Studio is $17/month — just $2 more than 17hats' Essentials plan — but includes all features. No tiers, no upgrade prompts, no feature gates. Every photographer gets gallery integrations, shot lists, full automation, and photography-specific workflows at the single price.

The real price comparison depends on which 17hats tier you need. If you need automation (and most photographers do), you are looking at 17hats Standard at $30/month — nearly double Argo's $17/month. At that price point, Argo offers significantly more photography-specific value.

## The Generalist vs. Specialist Divide

17hats is designed to work for any solopreneur. The same platform that a photographer uses is also used by accountants, fitness coaches, real estate agents, and wedding planners. This means every feature is designed to be broadly applicable rather than deeply useful for any single profession.

When you set up 17hats, you start with generic templates, generic workflow steps, and generic terminology. You then spend time customizing these for photography: renaming stages, rewriting email templates, building photography-specific questionnaires, and configuring workflows that match how photography businesses actually operate.

Argo Studio skips this customization entirely. When you sign up and indicate you are a wedding photographer, you get wedding photography workflows, wedding questionnaire templates, wedding contract language, and wedding-specific automation sequences. The same applies for portrait, commercial, family, and event photography specialties. The system understands the difference between a mini session and a full wedding day because it was built exclusively for photographers.

## Gallery Integrations

This is the clearest differentiation. Argo Studio integrates natively with Pic-Time, CloudSpot, ShootProof, and Pixieset. Gallery delivery events flow into your CRM and trigger automated follow-ups, review requests, and project status updates. Your CRM and your gallery platform work as a connected system.

17hats has no gallery integrations whatsoever. As a general small business tool, gallery platforms are not part of its integration ecosystem. A fitness coach does not need Pic-Time integration, so it does not exist. Photographers using 17hats manage all gallery-related workflows manually — tracking delivery, following up on gallery views, requesting reviews, and updating project statuses by hand.

For a photographer shooting 50+ sessions per year, this manual gallery management adds hours of administrative work that Argo eliminates automatically.

## Shot List Management

Argo Studio includes built-in shot list management with templates for different session types, client collaboration features, and mobile access for shoot-day reference. Wedding photographers can build formal family grouping lists and share them with couples. Portrait photographers can create pose and location checklists.

17hats does not have a shot list feature. It was not designed with photographer workflows in mind, so session-day tools like shot lists are outside its scope. Photographers using 17hats rely on Google Docs, Notion, or standalone apps for shot list management — adding another tool to an already fragmented tech stack.

## Bookkeeping and Financial Tools

One area where 17hats has a genuine advantage is its built-in bookkeeping functionality. The platform includes basic expense tracking, income categorization, and tax preparation features. For solopreneurs who want to avoid a separate accounting tool, 17hats can handle basic financial management.

Argo Studio includes revenue tracking and financial reporting but does not position itself as a bookkeeping tool. Most photographers using Argo pair it with QuickBooks, Wave, or a similar accounting platform for comprehensive financial management.

If you want basic bookkeeping bundled with your CRM, 17hats includes it. If you already use a dedicated accounting tool, this feature is less relevant and does not justify choosing 17hats over Argo's photography-specific capabilities.

## Workflow Automation

Argo Studio's automation is photography-specific with pre-configured sequences for each stage of the photographer-client lifecycle: inquiry follow-up, booking confirmation, pre-session questionnaires, day-of reminders, gallery delivery, review requests, and referral programs. These automations understand photography context and require minimal customization.

17hats offers general business automation that you configure for your specific use case. The automation engine is capable — you can set up time-based triggers, action-based triggers, and multi-step sequences. But every automation needs to be built from scratch because 17hats does not know what a "gallery delivery" or a "shot list completion" event means. You are teaching a general tool to behave like a photography tool.

For photographers who want automation to work immediately, Argo's pre-built photography workflows deliver instant value. With 17hats, you invest time building what Argo includes by default.

## Comparison with HoneyBook and Dubsado

17hats competes most directly with HoneyBook ($19/month) and Dubsado ($20/month) as generalist CRMs for creative professionals. All three serve broad audiences and lack photography-specific features like gallery integrations and shot lists.

Among these generalist options, HoneyBook has the best mobile app and user interface, Dubsado offers the deepest customization, and 17hats has the lowest entry price with bundled bookkeeping. But none of them match Argo Studio's photography-specific feature set at $17/month. If you are a photographer comparing all four platforms, the question is whether you want a tool built for everyone or a tool built for you.

## Who Should Choose Argo Studio

Choose Argo Studio if you want a CRM built exclusively for photographers at $17/month that understands your workflow from day one. Argo is ideal if you use gallery platforms like Pic-Time or ShootProof and want automated, gallery-connected workflows. It is the right choice if you would rather spend your time photographing clients than configuring a general-purpose tool to work for photography. Photographers who want shot list management, photography-specific templates, and a system that speaks their language will find Argo is built for them.

## Who Should Choose 17hats

Choose 17hats if budget is your absolute top priority and you want the lowest possible entry price for a CRM. 17hats is also a reasonable choice if you run a multi-service business where photography is just one of several offerings and you need a tool that is equally generic across all your services. It may be the right fit if you want basic bookkeeping bundled with your CRM and do not want a separate accounting tool. Be prepared to invest time configuring its general features for photography-specific use.

## Common Pain Points Photographers Face with HoneyBook

As photographers grow their businesses, several HoneyBook limitations tend to surface repeatedly. These are not edge cases — they are patterns reported across photography communities, forums, and reviews.

### It Is Not Photography-Specific

HoneyBook serves florists, planners, DJs, designers, and dozens of other creative fields. That breadth means the platform cannot go deep on any single profession's needs. Photographers miss features like shot lists, timeline builders, second shooter management, and gear tracking — tools that a photography-first platform can offer natively.

When your CRM does not understand the difference between an engagement session and a wedding day, you end up building workarounds. Those workarounds cost time, and time is what solo photographers have the least of.

### Expensive for What You Get

HoneyBook's pricing starts at $19/month on an annual plan and scales up from there. For photographers who only need core CRM functionality — leads, contracts, invoices, scheduling — paying a premium for features designed for other industries feels wasteful. Many photographers also subscribe to Dubsado or other tools alongside HoneyBook to fill gaps, compounding the cost.

At $17/month, Argo delivers photography-specific functionality without the filler features you will never use.

### Limited Gallery Integrations

Photographers live in their gallery platforms. Whether you deliver through Pic-Time, ShootProof, CloudSpot, or Pixieset, your gallery system is central to your client experience. HoneyBook does not integrate deeply with these platforms, which means you are manually copying links, tracking delivery status outside your CRM, and losing visibility into the client journey after the shoot.

This disconnect between your CRM and your gallery platform creates a gap in your workflow that costs hours every week.
